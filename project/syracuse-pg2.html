The Facts

Everyday, local Syracuse residents are hit hard with the harsh reality that is reflectant on poverty in America.

View these interactive graphs to learn more.

  1. In 2017 the majority of degrees awarded at institutions in Syracuse, NY were to White students
  2. These 4,745 degrees mean that there were 6.93 times more White students then the next closest race/ethnicity group
  3. When the gap between race and education is this large, it makes it more difficult to combat poverty.
  1. From 2016 to 2017, employment in Syracuse, NY grew at a rate of 0.118% , from 58.7k employees to 58.7k employees. We are looking to help to increase these numbers even more.
  2. The most common employment sectors for those who live in Syracuse, NY, are Health Care & Social Assistance (11,587 people), Educational Services (9,247 people), and Retail Trade (6,594 people).
  3. ** Census data is tagged to a residential address, not a work address, so some of these residents may live in Syracuse, NY and work somewhere else.**

  1. 32.6% of the population for whom poverty status is determined in Syracuse, NY (42.7k out of 131k people) live below the poverty line, a number that is higher than the national average of 13.1%
  2. The largest demographic living in poverty are Females 18 - 24.
  3. The poverty rate is followed by Females 25 - 34 and then Males 18 - 24.
  1. In 2017, the median household income of the 55.6k households in Syracuse, NY grew to $34,716 from the previous year's value of $32,704.
  2. However, The largest share of households have an income below the $10k range.
